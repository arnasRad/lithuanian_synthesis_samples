Audio samples from master's thesis "Lithuanian Speech Synthesis Using Neural Networks"

Author: Arnas Radzevičius

Abstract:

This master’s thesis work proposes an approach to using stressed text instead of phonemes for TTS neural network inputs to solve the pronunciation problem of synthesized speech for higher-degree phonemic orthography languages. Tacotron 2 and VITS neural network architectures were used to train neural networks on multiple Lithuanian language datasets. Three single-speaker Lithuanian language speech corpora were collected to be used for the model training experiments, totaling 6, 27, and 92 hours of speech data, respectively. Finally, a survey is conducted to calculate MOS and evaluate each trained TTS neural network.

Furthermore, the initial experimental results of training a neural network-based accentuation model are detailed. The accentuation model is required as a pre-processing component for the TTS model to solve the synthesized speech pronunciation problem. The best-trained model achieves an accuracy (character-level) of 93%, but the model is not practical since it assigns stress marks to all the letters in the input sequence instead of assigning a single pitch accent for each word in the sequence.

The readers are provided a link to a website demonstrating the speech samples generated by the developed synthesizers. Also, the base pre-trained neural network models are provided in the links below.

Samples

Below are the samples generated by Tacotron 2 and VITS models trained on three Lithuanian language datasets: Aurimas, Giedrius, and Vytautas. The datasets are described in more detail in the authors' master's thesis

For the master's thesis work, nine models were trained in total. That includes six Tacotron 2 models trained using stressed and non-stressed text labels on the datasets mentioned above. Three VITS models were trained using stressed text labels on the same datasets. The non-stressed samples were generated by feeding the stressed models non-stressed text in the VITS case.

Samples generated by 15 speech sources are presented on this page. These are 12 TTS neural network sources and 3 ground-truth sources from the test sets of the Aurimas, Giedrius, and Vytautas datasets. The samples are grouped by speaker voice (Aurimas, Giedrius, and Vytautas)

Lastly, utterances generated by other three best-known Lithuanian language synthesizers are presented. These synthesizers are LIEPA's Edvardas, Microsoft's Leonas, and VDU synthesizer.
