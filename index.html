Audio samples from master's thesis "Lithuanian Speech Synthesis Using Neural Networks"

Author: Arnas Radzevičius
Abstract: This work focuses on speech synthesis for low-resource languages, specifically - Lithuanian language. An approach to use stressed text instead of phonemes as TTS neural network inputs is proposed. Tacotron 2 and VITS neural network models were trained on multiple Lithuanian language datasets. For the purpose of this work, a 92-hour speech corpus was collected. Also, in collaboration with AAI Labs, two additional speech datasets (6-hour and 10-hour, respectively) were composed. The readers are provided a link to the speech synthesized by the models trained for purposes of this work to establish a subjective opinion about the quality of each model. Furthermore, initial experimental results of training a neural network-based accentuation model are provided. The accentuation model is required as a pre-processing component for the TTS model to solve the synthesized speech pronunciation problem. The best-trained model achieves accuracy (character-level) of 93%, but the model is not useful since it assigns stress marks all the letters in the input sequence, instead of assigning a single pitch accent for each word in the sequence.
